# Real-Life Copy Reference

Use this for Xiaohongshu-style posts, social captions, product copy, newsletters, short-video scripts, community posts, and writing that needs to face real readers in daily life.

## What real copy is

Real copy is not a pile of hooks, emojis, and pain points. It is writing that knows:

- Who is reading and what situation they are in.
- What the reader already believes, worries about, wants, or resists.
- What concrete action or recognition the text should create.
- Which details are true enough to carry trust.

Good copy often sounds like someone with taste and firsthand contact, not a template optimized for every platform at once.

## Diagnose fake copy

Look for:

- Generic opening anxiety: "你是不是也..." without a specific scene.
- Overpromised outcomes: "看完立刻改变", "三天逆袭", "普通人必看".
- Fake intimacy: "姐妹们听我说" when the voice has no real relation to the reader.
- Empty contrast: "不是方法不对，而是你没找准底层逻辑."
- Pseudo-data: unsourced percentages, invented cases, vague "很多人反馈".
- Stacked adjectives: "高级、松弛、治愈、氛围感、绝绝子" without evidence.
- Platform costume: forcing hashtags, bullets, and slogans when the content needs a plain note.

## Revision procedure

1. Identify the real reader: student, researcher, buyer, colleague, friend, parent, patient, beginner, expert, local resident, etc.
2. Identify the real scene: before a deadline, in a store, after trying a product, during revision, commuting, cooking, recovering, planning.
3. Replace generic hook with a scene, tension, or honest claim.
4. Keep only claims that can be supported by the text.
5. Use details that change the reader's picture: time, object, hesitation, small failure, comparison, price, location, material, process.
6. End with a practical next step, a remembered detail, or a quiet conclusion. Do not force a slogan.

## Xiaohongshu and social post patterns

Use the platform's rhythm only when appropriate:

- Short paragraphs are useful, but not every sentence needs its own line.
- A title can be specific without shouting.
- Tags should describe real categories, not spam.
- First-person experience is valuable only if true. If experience is unavailable, write as observation, guide, or analysis.
- Keep one main promise per post.

## Before and after examples

### Template-like

```text
不是你不会写论文，而是你没有掌握底层逻辑。今天分享三个方法，让你的论文瞬间高级起来。
```

### More real

```text
很多论文卡住，不是因为材料太少，而是因为每一段都在介绍背景。先问一句：这一段到底要帮读者解决哪个判断？如果答不上来，句子再漂亮也会散。
```

### Template-like

```text
这款杯子真的太高级了，通勤、办公、健身都很适合，颜值和实用性兼具。
```

### More real

```text
我会把它放在办公桌左手边，因为单手能打开，杯口也不容易溅水。它不算轻，通勤背很久会有点存在感；但如果主要在办公室用，这个重量反而稳。
```

## Grounding options when details are missing

If the user provides only abstract claims, do not invent lived scenes. Ask for one or two missing details when necessary, or write a conservative version:

```text
如果你有真实使用场景，可以把这里的"办公桌/通勤/杯口"换成你的细节。现在我先保留为不虚构的判断。
```
