# Literary Writing Reference

Use this for essays, reflective prose, speeches, prefaces, narrative passages, cultural criticism, and academic writing that needs human texture without becoming decorative.

## What to learn from literary writers

Do not copy a writer's recognizable style. Extract portable craft:

- **Rhythm**: Vary sentence length and syntactic weight. Let long sentences carry thought or observation; let short sentences interrupt, land, or leave room.
- **Scene**: Anchor abstraction in a place, gesture, object, weather, body position, document, classroom, street, archive, desk, screen, or remembered moment.
- **Perspective**: Decide who is seeing. A text can stand inside the writer's doubt, the reader's confusion, a participant's memory, or an observer's distance.
- **Silence**: Do not explain every implication. Leave a controlled gap where the reader can infer.
- **Sensory detail**: Use touch, light, sound, temperature, spatial relation, and material texture only when they reveal judgment or situation.

## How literary craft removes AI flavor

AI-like prose often names emotions and conclusions too early. Literary prose often delays naming. It lets arrangement do part of the thinking.

Replace:

- Summary before experience.
- Concept without scene.
- Perfectly balanced paragraphs.
- All-purpose adjectives.
- Endings that explain what the reader should feel.

With:

- A concrete entrance before the general claim.
- One detail that changes the reader's angle.
- Uneven but intentional rhythm.
- A perspective that limits what can be known.
- An ending that stops at the right pressure point.

## Revision procedure

1. Identify the piece's center of gravity: argument, memory, observation, confession, criticism, or invitation.
2. Choose one governing perspective. Do not jump between grand overview and intimate confession unless the shift is purposeful.
3. Add scene only where abstraction is floating. A scene should carry meaning, not decorate the paragraph.
4. Replace emotional labels with readable evidence: action, pause, object, bodily reaction, or setting.
5. Cut explanatory endings. Let the last sentence either return to a concrete image, sharpen a thought, or leave a precise silence.
6. Preserve the user's intellectual content. Literary humanization should not blur the claim.

## Useful transformations

### From abstract to situated

```text
AI-like: 这段经历让我深刻意识到个体在时代洪流中的渺小与坚韧。
More literary: 那天晚上我一直坐在走廊的长椅上，手机屏幕亮了又暗。后来我才意识到，所谓时代，有时候就是一个人不知道该先回复哪一条消息。
```

### From over-explained to restrained

```text
AI-like: 这不仅是一种失落，更是一种成长，标志着我对人生有了新的理解。
More literary: 我没有再解释。杯子里的水已经凉了，窗外的声音也慢慢低下去。
```

### From smooth to rhythmic

```text
AI-like: 我们需要重新审视教育的意义，因为教育不仅关乎知识传递，也关乎人的成长和社会的发展。
More literary: 教育当然传递知识。但有些时刻，它更像一次缓慢的校准：一个人怎样看见别人，怎样承认自己的局限，又怎样在这些局限里继续往前走。
```

## Boundaries

- Do not add dramatic pain, nostalgia, childhood memories, illness, family scenes, or intimate details unless the user provides them.
- Do not turn academic prose into lyrical prose when the genre requires clarity.
- Do not use sensory detail as perfume. If a detail does not change the thought, remove it.
- Do not overuse "我". Perspective can be present without constant self-reference.
