# Academic Writing Reference

Use this for Chinese academic prose, research collaboration, papers, proposals, abstracts, literature reviews, reviews, and seminar-style argument.

## What "human" means in academic prose

Human academic writing is not chatty. It usually has:

- A real problem pressure: the paragraph exists because something is contested, under-described, misread, or worth distinguishing.
- A visible reasoning path: claims unfold through evidence, concepts, counter-positions, and limits.
- Calibrated confidence: strong where evidence is strong, cautious where inference is partial.
- Texture from concepts and examples, not from decorative adjectives.
- Paragraphs with jobs: introduce a problem, narrow a concept, compare positions, read evidence, handle objection, or state implication.

## Learn from philosophers and literary writers without copying them

Extract arrangement patterns:

- **Conceptual tightening**: begin with an ordinary claim, expose its hidden assumption, then restate the problem more sharply.
- **Dialectical pressure**: let an opposing view appear in its strongest form before responding.
- **Patient qualification**: add limits because the object is complex, not because the writer is afraid to take a stance.
- **Concrete pivot**: after abstraction, return to a scene, term, passage, method, dataset, or institutional condition.
- **Paragraph turn**: make the last sentence change the reader's angle rather than merely summarize.
- **Sentence weight variation**: place dense conceptual sentences beside shorter clarifying sentences.

Do not produce decorative imitation such as archaic diction, famous-author cadence, or signature metaphors. The goal is intellectual arrangement, not cosplay.

## Revision procedure

1. Name the central claim in one sentence. If it cannot be named, revise the structure first.
2. Mark each paragraph's function. Merge paragraphs with the same job; split paragraphs doing too many jobs.
3. Replace generic topic sentences with problem-bearing sentences.
4. Check evidence order: concept -> material -> interpretation, or material -> puzzle -> concept. Avoid concept -> concept -> concept.
5. Reduce "overview voice": phrases like "本文将从三个方面展开" are only useful when the reader genuinely needs a map.
6. Remove exaggerated contribution claims unless supported by the work.
7. Add uncertainty where method, sample, archive, translation, or interpretation has limits.

## Common academic rewrites

### Weak AI-like pattern

```text
随着社会的发展，X越来越受到关注。本文从A、B、C三个方面探讨X的重要意义，并揭示其深层价值。
```

### More human academic direction

```text
关于X的讨论并不缺少结论，真正含混的是这些结论依赖的前提。本文因此不急于把X概括为某种趋势，而是先回到A中的具体材料，说明B如何改变了我们理解C的方式。
```

## Academic collaboration tone

For emails, peer comments, coauthor notes, and advisor-student collaboration:

- Be direct about the task and uncertainty.
- Prefer "我这里的判断是..." over universalizing language.
- State what changed, why it changed, and what still needs a decision.
- Avoid performative politeness that obscures action.

Example direction:

```text
我把第二节的顺序调了一下：先处理概念界定，再进入案例。这样做的好处是读者不会在案例部分才发现我们对X的定义其实发生了变化。第三段我还不确定，尤其是Y和Z之间的过渡，可能需要你再看一眼。
```
